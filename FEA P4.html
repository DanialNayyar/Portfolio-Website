Modelling a Steel Specimen under Load

Modeled and simulated a dynamic fracture test, under SHPB conditions, to analyse material failuer in ABAQUS .



üîß Overview

This project explored the dynamic tensile behaviour of a steel speciment under Split Hopkins Pressure Bar (SHPB) conditions using the ABAQUS Software. A M-shaped geomrety was made in SolidWorks and then imported into ABAQUS where it was subjected to compressive displacement. Elemet deletion was also implemented to replicate the fracture behaviour. This project's main focus was on stress concentration, strain, displacement and dynamic yielding in the gauge section of the specimen.

üéØ Objectives

  • Simulate compressive dynamic loading of a steel specimen with the the relevant boundary conditions
  • Investigate the material response under the dynamic conditions inluding yielding and softening
  • Analyse the von Mises Stress, plastic strain (PEEQ), displacement and active yield regions
  • Plot stress-strain relation at the gauge sections
  • Analyse sources of simulation error and their effects

      This project demonstrated the importance or realistic boundary condtions in order to replicate physical test setups. Mesh size and Mesh type influence was also observed in terms of stress concentration accuracy. The hexahedral mesh, captured the major effects but did struggle with curved edge which suggested that Tetrahedral mesh elements yield better results. The small error margin of 1.2% validates the experimental setup
